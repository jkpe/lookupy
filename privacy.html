Privacy Policy

Lookupy · Effective March 23, 2026

This policy describes how Lookupy (“the app”) and the website lookupy.top handle information. Lookupy is a DNS lookup tool that uses DNS over HTTPS (DoH).

Information we do not collect

We do not require an account. We do not sell your personal information. We do not send push notifications. We do not use third-party analytics or advertising SDKs in the app to track you across other companies’ apps or websites.

DNS lookups and resolvers

When you query a domain name, that query is sent from your device to the public DNS resolver you select in the app (for example Cloudflare, Google, or another provider you choose), using encrypted HTTPS. Those operators may process queries according to their own privacy policies. We do not operate those resolvers and do not receive a separate copy of your queries on our servers for normal lookup use.

App and website

The iOS app loads the Lookupy web experience from lookupy.top inside a web view. A small cookie may be set so the site can tell that you are using the app (for example to adjust behavior). Standard web technologies may apply as on any website you visit.

Links that leave Lookupy may open in your browser; those sites have their own policies.

Data from Apple

Apple may collect diagnostic, usage, or crash data according to your device settings and Apple’s privacy policy. We receive only what Apple chooses to share with developers (for example aggregated crash reports if you opt in).

Children

Lookupy is not directed at children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from them.

Changes

We may update this policy from time to time. The “Effective” date at the top will change when we do.

Contact

Questions about this policy: visit lookupy.top (the same URL we list as support in the App Store).
